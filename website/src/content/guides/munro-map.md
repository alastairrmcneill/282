Scotland's 282 Munros aren't scattered evenly — they cluster into a dozen great mountain
areas, each with its own character. Here's the map of Munro country, region by region.
Every region links to its full list; every Munro has its own map, photo and route links.

*For one interactive map of all 282 — colour-coded by what you've climbed — get the
[282 app](/).*

## [Fort William](/regions/fort-william) — 67 Munros

The heavyweight. [Ben Nevis](/munros/ben-nevis) (1,345m, Britain's highest), the arc of
the Mamores, the Grey Corries, Glen Coe's scrambly ridges and the remote hills of Knoydart
— nowhere else packs in so many big days.

## [Cairngorms](/regions/cairngorms) — 54 Munros

Britain's arctic plateau: five of the six highest Munros, including
[Ben Macdui](/munros/ben-macdui) (1,309m) and [Braeriach](/munros/braeriach) (1,296m),
rise above vast gravel plateaux and ancient pine forest.

## [Perthshire](/regions/perthshire) — 28 Munros

The gentle(r) south: [Ben Lawers](/munros/ben-lawers) (1,214m) above Loch Tay, the
Tarmachan ridge, and the perfect cone of [Schiehallion](/munros/schiehallion) — many a
first Munro.

## [Kintail](/regions/kintail) — 24 Munros

Ridge-walking heaven around Glen Shiel: the Five Sisters, the South Glen Shiel Ridge
(seven Munros in a day) and the Forcan Ridge on The Saddle.

## [Ullapool](/regions/ullapool) — 24 Munros

The Fannichs, the Fisherfield wilderness — home of remote
[A' Mhaighdean](/munros/a-mhaighdean) — and the pinnacled castle of An Teallach.

## [Argyll](/regions/argyll) — 19 Munros

[Ben Lui](/munros/ben-lui) (1,131m), the Arrochar Alps' rugged
[Ben Ime](/munros/beinn-ime), and [Ben Cruachan](/munros/ben-cruachan)'s horseshoe above
Loch Awe.

## [Loch Ness](/regions/loch-ness) — 17 Munros

North of the Great Glen: [Carn Eige](/munros/carn-eige) (1,183m), the highest ground north
of it, plus the great Affric and Mullardoch ridges.

## [Torridon](/regions/torridon) — 17 Munros

Sandstone monsters — Liathach, Beinn Eighe, Beinn Alligin — rising straight from sea
lochs. Small in number, giant in character.

## [Loch Lomond](/regions/loch-lomond) — 14 Munros

The most accessible group from Glasgow: [Ben Lomond](/munros/ben-lomond), the Crianlarich
hills around [Ben More](/munros/ben-more) (1,174m), and Loch Earn's
[Ben Vorlich](/munros/ben-vorlich-loch-earn).

## [Islands](/regions/islands) — 13 Munros

Twelve on Skye's Black Cuillin — including the notorious
[Inaccessible Pinnacle](/munros/inaccessible-pinnacle) — plus
[Ben More](/munros/ben-more-mull) on Mull, the only island Munro outside Skye.

## [Angus](/regions/angus) — 3 Munros

The rolling hills above the Angus glens: [Driesh](/munros/driesh),
[Mayar](/munros/mayar) and [Lochnagar](/munros/lochnagar)'s neighbouring plateau country
on the Deeside boundary.

## [Sutherland](/regions/sutherland) — 2 Munros

The far north's lonely pair: [Ben Hope](/munros/ben-hope), the most northerly Munro, and
[Ben Klibreck](/munros/ben-klibreck) rising alone from the Flow Country.
