Only two mountain areas in Britain reach above 1,200 metres: the Nevis range above Fort
William and the Cairngorm plateau. Between them they own nine of the ten highest Munros —
here's the full top ten, ranked.

## 1. [Ben Nevis](/munros/ben-nevis) — 1,345m (4,413ft)

The roof of the British Isles. "The Ben" hides its magnificence from the tourist track —
its true glory is the two-mile wall of cliffs on the north face, Britain's biggest. The
adventurous bag it via the Carn Mor Dearg arête, one of the finest ridge walks in the
country.

## 2. [Ben Macdui](/munros/ben-macdui) — 1,309m (4,295ft)

The Cairngorms' highest point and Britain's second summit, rising from a vast sub-arctic
plateau. Home of the Big Grey Man (Am Fear Liath Mòr), Scotland's most famous mountain
ghost — in thick mist on that featureless plateau, you'll understand the legend.

## 3. [Braeriach](/munros/braeriach) — 1,296m (4,252ft)

The great western wall of the Lairig Ghru. Its summit curls around An Garbh Choire, the
snowiest corrie in Scotland, where snow patches survive most summers.

## 4. [Cairn Toul](/munros/cairn-toul) — 1,291m (4,236ft)

Braeriach's elegant neighbour across the corrie rim — a proper pointed summit by Cairngorm
standards, and a long way from any road: earn it via the Lairig Ghru.

## 5. [Sgor an Lochain Uaine](/munros/sgor-an-lochain-uaine) — 1,258m (4,127ft)

The "Angel's Peak", promoted to full Munro status in 1997 — the newest member of the
4,000ft club, perched above its little green lochan.

## 6. [Cairn Gorm](/munros/cairn-gorm) — 1,245m (4,085ft)

The mountain that named the range. The funicular and ski infrastructure make it the
most-visited high summit in Scotland, but its northern corries hold some of Britain's best
winter climbing.

## 7. [Aonach Beag](/munros/aonach-beag-nevis-range) — 1,234m (4,049ft)

"Little ridge" — little only beside its neighbour. A shy giant hidden behind Ben Nevis
with a huge, rarely seen east face.

## 8. [Aonach Mor](/munros/aonach-mor) — 1,220m (4,003ft)

Home of the Nevis Range gondola on its northern flank, yet the summit plateau feels a
world away. Usually bagged with Aonach Beag in one high traverse.

## 9. [Carn Mor Dearg](/munros/carn-mor-dearg) — 1,220m (4,003ft)

The "big red cairn" — a graceful pink-granite pyramid joined to Ben Nevis by the famous
CMD arête. Many baggers call this the best way up the Ben, full stop.

## 10. [Ben Lawers](/munros/ben-lawers) — 1,214m (3,983ft)

The highest mountain in the southern Highlands, looming over Loch Tay. Botanists rate its
arctic-alpine flora the richest in Britain; baggers rate the seven-Munro Lawers ridge one
of the best big days in the south.

## The 4,000-footers challenge

The top nine on this list are Scotland's only summits over 4,000 feet, split between the
Nevis range and the Cairngorms. Strong walkers link all nine in a single brutal 24-hour
push; mortals take a glorious long weekend.
