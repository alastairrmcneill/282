Every Munro bagger remembers their first. Choose well and you'll come home tired, happy and
already planning number two. These ten are the classic starters: good paths, honest
navigation, and summits that deliver far more view than they demand in effort.

*Times are typical round-trip estimates for a steady walker in summer conditions. Always
check the [weather](/guides/munro-bagging-for-beginners) before you go.*

## 1. [The Cairnwell](/munros/the-cairnwell) — 933m, 2–3 hours

The gentlest introduction in the book. The Glenshee Ski Centre car park sits at ~665m, so
you're already two-thirds of the way up before you take a step.

## 2. [Carn Aosda](/munros/carn-aosda) — 917m, 2–3 hours

The Cairnwell's neighbour, and usually climbed with it in a single short day on ski-centre
tracks. Two Munros before lunch — an encouraging start to any round.

## 3. [Carn a' Gheoidh](/munros/carn-a-gheoidh) — 975m, add 2 hours

The third of the Glenshee trio. Add it to the first two for a very achievable three-Munro
day — and an early "multi-Munro day" badge in the [282 app](/).

## 4. [Schiehallion](/munros/schiehallion) — 1,083m, 4–6 hours

The "fairy hill of the Caledonians" is many people's first Munro, and for good reason: a
superbly maintained path, a striking conical profile, and a place in scientific history —
the 1774 "weighing the world" experiment was conducted on its slopes.

## 5. [Ben Lomond](/munros/ben-lomond) — 974m, 4–6 hours

The most southerly Munro and the closest to Glasgow. The "tourist path" from Rowardennan is
clear all the way, with Loch Lomond glittering below the whole climb.

## 6. [Ben Chonzie](/munros/ben-chonzie) — 931m, 4–5 hours

A gentle track leads almost the whole way from Glen Lednock near Crieff. Famous for its
mountain hares — in winter you might see dozens.

## 7. [Mount Keen](/munros/mount-keen) — 939m, 5–6 hours

Scotland's most easterly Munro, reached by the historic Mounth track from Glen Esk. Long
but never steep, with a real sense of crossing old drove country.

## 8. [Meall nan Tarmachan](/munros/meall-nan-tarmachan) — 1,043m, 4–5 hours

A high start on the Ben Lawers road and an entertaining, twisty ridge. The full Tarmachan
Ridge is one of the best easy scrambles in the southern Highlands — or just do the Munro
and retrace your steps.

## 9. [Beinn Ghlas](/munros/beinn-ghlas) — 1,103m, with Ben Lawers 5–7 hours

Climbed en route to [Ben Lawers](/munros/ben-lawers), the highest peak in the southern
Highlands. The path from the NTS car park (starting ~450m up) bags you both in one
straightforward day.

## 10. [Ben Wyvis](/munros/ben-wyvis) — 1,046m, 5–6 hours

The great whaleback of the north, an easy drive from Inverness. A well-built path leads to
a broad mossy plateau summit with views across the whole of Easter Ross.

## Before you go

Even "easy" Munros are serious hills in bad weather — pack properly and check the
forecast. Our [beginner's guide](/guides/munro-bagging-for-beginners) covers everything
you need.

And when you get back down: open [282](/), mark it complete, and start the list. One down,
[281 to go](/munros).
